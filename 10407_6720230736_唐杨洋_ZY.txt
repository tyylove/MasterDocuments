摘要
自动驾驶列车是轨道交通领域的重要研究方向，列车运行过程中对前方环境的感知直接关系到行车安全与稳定。当前环境监测主要依赖视频监控与人工巡检，存在覆盖范围有限、成本高及实时性不足等问题，也难以实现站点间的持续监测。基于车载设备的视觉感知技术能够在运行过程中持续获取前方场景信息，并通过图像处理技术和视觉算法对轨道结构及障碍物进行识别，为环境感知提供数据支持。然而，轨道场景纹理复杂、目标尺度差异大，加之车载设备算力受限，使模型在精度与效率之间进行平衡。针对上述问题，本文以轨道环境视觉感知方法为背景，开展轨道分割与障碍物检测研究，并依托兴国永磁磁浮列车运行场景进行实验，构建兼顾精度、实时性与计算资源适配的感知方法，以提升列控系统的障碍物识别能力。本文主要工作如下：
（1）针对轨道区域分割任务中模型精度与计算效率难以兼顾的问题，设计了一种融合多尺度特征与多通道注意力的轻量级轨道分割网络LiteRailSegNet(Lightweight Rail Segmentation Network)。该网络利用部分卷积构建轻量化特征提取主干，以降低模型参数量与计算开销；设计多尺度边缘上采样特征增强网络，通过上采样与边缘增强机制强化并融合轨道中的多尺度特征；构建多通道注意力解码网络，将编码特征使用不同通道处理并结合双重注意力，提高模型对细节纹理与高层语义信息的解码能力。实验表明，LiteRailSegNet的模型参数仅有5.29M，计算开销5.2GFLOPs，符合当前的车载计算设备要求，在Railsem19数据集上取得86.07% mIoU与117.8 FPS的推理速度；在UAV-RS数据集上区域交并比达到90.05%，线交并比达到65.45%；在自建MTSD数据集上mIoU达到95.56%，在多个数据集上的结果表明该方法在保持较低模型复杂度的同时具有良好的分割精度与实时性能。
（2）在提取轨道区域的基础上，针对轨道区域中障碍物尺度变化明显、小目标占比高以及车载设备对实时检测性能要求较高的问题，提出了一种基于分割先验与预训练特征的轨道交通障碍物实时检测方法RT-ODFormer(Rail Transit Obstacle Detection Former)。所提方法采用基于DINOv3自监督预训练的ConvNeXt作为主干网络，通过多层级特征提取获取不同尺度目标的特征信息，并设计两级特征聚焦传播网络实现不同尺度特征之间的交互与信息融合，以增强模型对不同尺度目标的感知能力；在障碍物解码阶段，引入基于Transformer的检测头解码结构，通过全局特征建模实现对障碍物类别与位置的联合预测。在多目标障碍物数据集与行人行为数据集上的实验结果表明，模型参数量仅4.51M，推理速度达到152.1 FPS。同时，多目标障碍物检测中五类目标上分别取得0.85、0.91、0.94、0.75和0.94的检测精度，在行人行为检测中取得63.1% mAP50和45.3% mAP50:95，模型在不同尺度目标检测中均具有较好的性能，其中小目标检测召回率达到84%，验证了所提方法在远距离障碍物感知与漏检方面的有效性。与多种检测方法相比，本方法在检测精度、计算复杂度与实时性之间取得了更好的平衡。
本文利用图像处理技术实现了从轨道区域结构感知和区域内障碍物检测，为列车避障系统中的视觉感知模块提供了一种兼顾实时性与准确性的技术方案，可以为轨道交通智能安全监测与自动驾驶列车环境感知系统的设计提供参考。

关键词：轨道交通系统；视觉感知；图像处理；轨道区域分割；障碍物检测

Abstract
Autonomous train operation has become an important research direction in the field of rail transit, where environmental perception during train operation is closely related to operational safety and stability. Current environmental monitoring methods mainly rely on video surveillance and manual inspection, which suffer from limited coverage, high labor costs, and insufficient real-time performance, making continuous monitoring between stations difficult to achieve. Vision-based perception technologies using onboard devices can continuously acquire forward-scene information during train operation. By combining image processing techniques with visual algorithms, track structures and obstacles can be identified, thereby providing essential data support for environmental perception. However, the complex textures of rail scenes, significant variations in target scales, and limited onboard computational resources make it challenging to balance model accuracy and efficiency. To address these issues, this thesis investigates rail-environment visual perception methods, focusing on rail segmentation and obstacle detection tasks. Experiments are conducted based on the operational scenarios of the Xingguo permanent-magnet maglev railway system. The objective is to develop perception methods that achieve a balance among accuracy, real-time performance, and computational efficiency, thereby improving the obstacle recognition capability of train control systems. The main contributions of this thesis are summarized as follows:
(1) Aiming at the problem that is difficulty to balance accuracy and computational efficiency in track region segmentation, a lightweight rail segmentation network named LiteRailSegNet (Lightweight Rail Segmentation Network) is proposed, which integrates multi-scale feature extraction and multi-channel attention mechanisms. A lightweight feature extraction backbone based on partial convolution is employed to reduce model parameters and computational overhead. In addition, a multi-scale edge-enhanced up sampling module is designed to strengthen and fuse multi-scale rail features through up sampling and edge enhancement operations. Furthermore, a multi-channel attention decoding network is constructed to process encoded features through different channel branches combined with dual-attention mechanisms, thereby improving the decoding capability for detailed textures and high-level semantic information. Experimental results demonstrate that LiteRailSegNet contains only 5.29M parameters with 5.2 GFLOPs, satisfying the computational requirements of onboard devices. The proposed method achieves 86.07% mIoU with an inference speed of 117.8 FPS on the RailSem19 dataset, 90.05% region IoU and 65.45% line IoU on the UAV-RS dataset, and 95.56% mIoU on the self-constructed MTSD dataset. Results on multiple datasets verify that the proposed method achieves favorable segmentation accuracy and real-time performance while maintaining low model complexity.
(2) Based on the extracted rail regions, a real-time rail-transit obstacle detection method named RT-ODFormer (Rail Transit Obstacle Detection Former) is proposed to address the challenges of significant object scale variation, a high proportion of small targets, and strict real-time requirements in complex rail scenarios. The proposed method employs a ConvNeXt backbone pretrained with the DINOv3 self-supervised learning strategy to extract multi-level semantic features for objects at different scales. Moreover, a two-level feature focus propagation network is designed to facilitate cross-scale feature interaction and information fusion, thereby enhancing the perception capability for multi-scale targets. During the obstacle decoding stage, a Transformer-based detection head is introduced to jointly predict obstacle categories and locations through global feature modeling. Experimental results demonstrate that RT-ODFormer contains only 4.51M parameters and achieves an inference speed of 152.1 FPS, satisfying the real-time requirements of rail transit visual perception tasks. In the multi-object obstacle detection task, the proposed method achieves detection accuracies of 0.85, 0.91, 0.94, 0.75, and 0.94 for five object categories, respectively. On the pedestrian behavior detection dataset, the model achieves 63.1% mAP50 and 45.3% mAP50:95. Furthermore, experiments on targets of different scales show that the proposed method maintains robust detection performance, with a recall rate of 84% for small objects, demonstrating its effectiveness in distant obstacle perception and missed-detection risk reduction. Compared with several mainstream detection methods, the proposed approach achieves a better balance among detection accuracy, computational complexity, and real-time performance.
In this thesis, image processing techniques are utilized to achieve both structural perception of rail regions and obstacle detection within rail areas. The proposed methods provide an effective technical solution for visual perception modules in train obstacle avoidance systems and can serve as a reference for the design of intelligent safety monitoring systems and autonomous train environmental perception systems in rail transit applications.

Key Words: Rail transit systems; Visual perception; Image processing; Track region segmentation; Obstacle detection
